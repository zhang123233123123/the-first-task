# Formal Questionnaire Draft for the CHI Creativity Experiment

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document provides a formal English draft of the questionnaire blocks for the CHI creativity experiment system. The wording is designed to be suitable for a research protocol, system design appendix, or a later Methods section. The draft is intentionally formal and standardized. It should be treated as a structured item bank for refinement, piloting, and scale validation.

Important methodological note:

- this draft is **not** claiming that every item below is copied verbatim from an existing validated instrument
- some blocks are anchored to established scales and should later be aligned more directly with the original instrument wording
- some blocks are intentionally **task-adapted** because the study requires state-like, task-specific measurement
- some blocks are **researcher-authored design-specific checks**, which is appropriate for manipulation checks and study-specific control items

For publication-facing use, this draft should therefore be read together with a source-mapping document that specifies which blocks are validated, adapted, or custom.

This draft follows the confirmed study structure:

- all participants complete two tasks
- both tasks are completed within the same study session
- the same experimental condition applies across both tasks
- AI interaction follows a system-pushed suggestions only design
- each task round is followed by its own post-task questionnaire


## 2. General Administration Notes

### 2.1 Baseline Questionnaire Timing

The baseline questionnaire should be administered after consent and general instructions, but before condition assignment becomes behaviorally relevant to the participant.

### 2.2 Post-Task Questionnaire Timing

The post-task questionnaire should be administered immediately after each task round. The instructions should explicitly anchor participant responses to the task they have just completed.

Recommended instruction:

> Please answer the following questions based on the task you have just completed.

### 2.3 Response Format

Unless otherwise specified, attitudinal items should use a 7-point Likert scale:

1. Strongly disagree  
2. Disagree  
3. Somewhat disagree  
4. Neither agree nor disagree  
5. Somewhat agree  
6. Agree  
7. Strongly agree

Where lower burden is preferred, a 5-point scale may be substituted, but scale format should remain consistent within each block.

### 2.4 Item Style Principles

The questionnaire should follow these wording rules:

- use clear first-person statements
- avoid academic jargon in participant-facing text
- avoid double-barreled items
- keep each item tied to either a trait, a specific task round, or a concrete system behavior
- avoid mixing satisfaction language into mediator or manipulation-check items


## 3. Baseline Questionnaire

## 3.1 Prior AI Use

Recommended response format:

- frequency items: 5-point ordinal frequency scale
- confidence items: 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> The following questions ask about your prior experience with generative AI tools.

Items:

1. How often do you use generative AI tools for any purpose?
2. How often do you use generative AI tools for writing-related tasks?
3. I am familiar with how to use generative AI tools to generate or improve written ideas.
4. I feel confident using generative AI tools to support creative work.
5. I have used generative AI tools for brainstorming or idea generation before.
6. I have used generative AI tools for revising or improving written content before.


## 3.2 Creative Writing Experience

Recommended response format:

- frequency or experience items: 5-point scale
- self-assessment items: 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> The following questions ask about your previous experience with creative writing.

Items:

1. How often do you engage in creative writing activities?
2. I have substantial prior experience writing short stories or fiction.
3. I have shared or published creative writing before.
4. I am familiar with writing tasks that require originality or imagination.
5. I consider myself an experienced creative writer.
6. I am comfortable generating written content under time constraints.


## 3.3 Baseline Creative Self-Efficacy

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements about your current creative ability.

Items:

1. I am capable of generating creative ideas in writing tasks.
2. I can develop an ordinary idea into something more original.
3. I am confident in my ability to produce imaginative written content.
4. I can improve a weak creative idea into a stronger one.


## 3.4 Need for Cognition

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements.

Items:

1. I prefer tasks that require careful thinking.
2. I enjoy working through complex problems.
3. I would rather think deeply about an issue than rely on a quick answer.
4. I find satisfaction in effortful thinking.
5. I like situations that require me to evaluate alternatives carefully.
6. I prefer tasks that challenge the way I think.


## 3.5 Locus of Control

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements.

Items:

1. My performance in demanding tasks usually depends on my own effort.
2. When I do well on a task, it is usually because of what I did.
3. I can usually influence the quality of my outcomes through my own decisions.
4. Success in difficult tasks is often outside my control.  
   Note: reverse-code if retained.
5. The choices I make usually have an important effect on the results I achieve.
6. I can usually improve an outcome if I change my approach.


## 3.6 Demographics

Recommended response format:

- fixed choice or short answer as appropriate

Recommended introductory text:

> The following questions are for research background purposes only.

Items:

1. What is your age?
2. What is your gender identity?
3. What is your highest level of completed education?
4. What is your current occupation or academic status?
5. What is your primary language?
6. Have you previously participated in studies involving generative AI?  
   Yes / No


## 4. Post-Task Questionnaire for Each Task Round

Recommended round-specific instruction:

> Please answer the following questions based only on the task you have just completed.


## 4.1 Knowledge of Cognition

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> During the task you have just completed, please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements.

Items:

1. During this task, I knew which AI suggestions were useful for my response.
2. I was aware of different ways I could develop my response.
3. I understood which parts of the task required my own judgment.
4. I could tell when the AI was helping me generate possibilities.
5. I recognized when an AI suggestion was not well suited to my task goals.
6. I understood what kinds of creative strategies were available to me during this task.


## 4.2 Regulation of Cognition

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> During the task you have just completed, please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements.

Items:

1. I evaluated AI suggestions before deciding whether to use them.
2. I monitored whether the AI-supported direction matched my task goals.
3. I changed my approach when the initial direction was not strong enough.
4. I checked whether my response was becoming too generic.
5. I actively revised or rejected ideas that did not fit well.
6. I deliberately considered the strengths and weaknesses of possible directions.
7. I tried to ensure that my final response remained original rather than routine.


## 4.3 Post-Task Creative Self-Efficacy

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Based on the task you have just completed, please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements.

Items:

1. I was capable of producing a creative response in this task.
2. I could improve a weak idea into something more original during this task.
3. I felt confident in my ability to shape the final creative outcome.
4. I was able to contribute creatively rather than simply follow the AI.


## 4.4 Cognitive Load

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale for a short form

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements about the task you have just completed.

Short-form items:

1. This task required substantial mental effort.
2. I had to think carefully before moving forward.
3. Completing this task was mentally demanding.

Optional expanded items:

4. I had to maintain a high level of concentration throughout the task.
5. The task felt cognitively effortful from beginning to end.


## 4.5 Provocateur Manipulation Check

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements about how the AI behaved during the task.

Items:

1. The AI challenged my assumptions.
2. The AI presented alternative perspectives rather than only helping me complete the task.
3. The AI prompted me to rethink my developing ideas.
4. The AI questioned or critiqued possible directions.
5. The AI behaved more like a challenger than a simple assistant.


## 4.6 Friction Manipulation Check

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements about how the interface behaved during the task.

Items:

1. The interface made me slow down before moving on.
2. The system required me to reflect before using ideas.
3. The interaction included deliberate pause points.
4. The design made me review or justify my choices rather than proceed immediately.
5. The interface was intentionally less frictionless than a typical AI tool.


## 4.7 Ownership and Authorship

Recommended response format:

- 7-point Likert scale

Recommended introductory text:

> Please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with the following statements about your relationship to the final response you produced.

Items:

1. I felt that the final response was truly mine.
2. I felt that I was directing the creative vision.
3. I shaped the final response rather than merely cleaning up AI output.


## 4.8 Attention and Recall Checks

### 4.8.1 Instructional Attention Check

Recommended response format:

- same Likert format as surrounding items

Recommended item:

1. To show that you are paying attention, please select "Somewhat disagree" for this item.

### 4.8.2 Task-Specific Recall Check

Recommended response format:

- multiple choice or fixed response

Recommended items:

1. During the task, were you required to review or justify an idea before proceeding?
2. Which of the following best describes how the AI behaved during this task?

Suggested answer format for Item 2:

- It mainly generated ideas without challenge
- It challenged or questioned possible directions
- It rewrote my response directly
- I am not sure


## 5. Task-Specific Wording Adjustments

The same questionnaire structure should be reused across both task rounds, but some wording should be adjusted slightly for the task domain.

### 5.1 Story Task Wording

Where appropriate, use:

- story
- story response
- story idea
- story direction

Example:

- "I knew which AI suggestions were useful for my story."

### 5.2 Metaphor Task Wording

Where appropriate, use:

- metaphor
- metaphor response
- figurative direction
- response to the prompt

Example:

- "I knew which AI suggestions were useful for my metaphor response."

## 7. Notes for Later Refinement

Before final deployment, this questionnaire draft should be further refined through:

- consistency review across task rounds
- item-length reduction if the full study burden is too high
- pilot testing for clarity
- alignment with any validated short forms that the final paper chooses to cite explicitly

The present draft is intended to provide a formal starting point for the questionnaire design, not a final validated instrument.
